\paragraph*{2.1} $\mathrm{e}^{x+1}$.

因为 
\[
	\begin{aligned}
	f(x+1)&=\lim_{n\to \infty}\left(1+\dfrac{x+2}{n-2}\right)^n\\
		  &=\lim_{n\to \infty}\left(1+\dfrac{x+2}{n-2}\right)^{\frac{n-2}{x+2}\cdot (x+2)\cdot\frac{n}{n-2}}\\
		  %&=\mathrm{e}^{x+2\lim_{n\to \infty}\frac{n}{n-2}}\\
		  &=\mathrm{e}^{x+2}
	\end{aligned}
\]
于是 $f(x)=\mathrm{e}^{x+1}$.
